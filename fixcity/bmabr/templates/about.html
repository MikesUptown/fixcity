What

fixcity.org is about taking small steps to fix your city; bike racks are the first issue we're taking on. fixcity.org Bike Racks is a mapping app for public participation in the planning of new bike rack locations.

Why

New York City has seen a dramatic increase in bicycling lately, but the lack of secure, legal bike parking is still a deterrent to many. NYC DOT's CityRacks program currently accepts one-off rack location requests from the public, but their process is better served by a bulk order to install en masse throughout an entire neighborhood. Bulk orders outsource planning work—time-intensive for DOT—to those who know the streets best: local residents, businesses, and community groups. It's a win-win situation and this app makes the process easier and more open.

Where

Our pilot project is focused on Brooklyn Community District 1 (BKCD1) and only suggestions in this area will be accepted in this trial period. Transportation Alternatives' Brooklyn Volunteer Committee and Neighbors Allied for Good Growth (NAG) are orchestrating the BKCD1 bulk order. This site will grow along with their process, enabling users to submit a bulk order to the CityRacks? program and keep track of its progress. We plan to scale this site for use throughout the city as soon as possible. Please let us know if you're interested in a bulk order of bike racks for your NYC community.

Who

fixcity.org is brought to you by the Livable Streets Initiative and The Open Planning Project in collaboration with Transportation Alternatives' Brooklyn Volunteer Committee and Neighbors Allied for Good Growth. Our 9.20.09 Bike Rack Hunt launch event is generously hosted by The Change You Want to See as part of Conflux. Special thanks to CityRacks Manager Jason Accime for his support and guidance.

What can you do?

Anyone can suggest new rack locations in the pilot area! To verify your rack and others for installment you’ll need an account. Contact us to get more involved!
